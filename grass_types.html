Grass Varieties

Bermuda

The most common grass in the area. We have two types of bermuda: Astro and 419 tiffway.

Zoysia

A dense grass with thick roots. Tolerates shade better than bermuda. We have 2 varieties: Myers and Matrella.

Fescue

A large blade thick grass. Likes shade but still needs some sunlight. Needs water.

Buffalo

A native grass to Oklahoma. This is a drought tolerant and low maintnence grass.

Bermuda

Astro
Hybrid Bermuda

Characteristics are a medium-sized blade and it maintains a beautiful dark-green color. This grass, like all Bermudas, likes sun and spreads very rapidly to give good coverage if water, fertilizer and mowing are applied on a regular basis

419
Tiffway

A fine bladed strain of Bermuda grass noted for its compact growth (sometimes referred to as dwarf). It is often selected for fine homes and golf course fairways as a “show-case” grass. To maintain the best appearance, grass clippings must be picked up when mowing. The use of water, fertilizer and regular mowing are needed for this grass to thrive just as in other Bermudas. This is a sun-loving grass and can tolerate both heat and cold.

Fescue

Black Beauty
Turf-Type Tall Fescue

Knowing that over half of the homes in any city have trees, we set out to find a shade loving grass that would provide an green lawn year-round. Johnathan Green suppies us with grass seed that is superior to any other variety we have grown before. Water is essential for fescue grass as well as special care in fertilizing and mowing.
